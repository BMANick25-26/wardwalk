How to do a ward walk

A clean, practical playbook you can use on shift. Prep, delivery, follow-up, and Pizza & Pay.

Start with prep Open WhatsApp for help
Keep it simple. You are there to listen, spot themes, and connect people to support.

What is a ward walk

Why you do it

  • Visibility and accessibility
  • Listen for themes
  • Signpost support
  • Build local intelligence

What it is not

  • Not confrontation
  • Not investigation
  • Not over-promising
  • Not naming individuals

How to prep

Quick checklist

  • Pick wards and timing
  • Choose one focus
  • Prepare 3–5 prompts
  • Bring QR/contact details
  • Know escalation routes
  • Agree roles if in a pair

Prompt questions

  • What’s making work harder right now?
  • Are training opportunities being lost?
  • If you fixed one thing, what would it be?

What to do during

  1. Introduce yourself and your role
  2. Check it’s a good time
  3. Listen for themes
  4. Note patterns, not names
  5. Offer next steps
  6. Invite follow-up

What to do post ward walk

Triage

  • Urgent safety issues
  • Important recurring themes
  • Local quick wins

Close the loop

  • Update staff
  • Track actions
  • Escalate appropriately

Pizza & Pay

Planning

  • Room, time, permissions
  • Budget and dietary needs
  • Promotion
  • Short talk track

Format

  1. Welcome
  2. Pay update
  3. Listening
  4. Next steps
