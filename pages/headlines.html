Headlines

Use these as factual talking points on ward walks.

Your pay buys less than when you started

Even in years with a “pay rise”, your salary has fallen behind inflation. In real terms, you are worse off than doctors doing the same job before you. This is cumulative and permanent unless reversed.

Responsibility has increased. Pay has not

Doctors are taking on senior clinical responsibility earlier. Registrars are often the most senior decision-maker on site overnight. The risk you carry has gone up, while real-terms pay has gone down.

Goodwill is being treated as unlimited

Staying late is routine. Rota gaps are normalised. Extra work is absorbed rather than paid. The system assumes doctors will continue to compensate for understaffing.

Training and jobs are no longer guaranteed

Competition ratios continue to rise while training numbers stay flat. Finishing foundation no longer guarantees progression. More doctors are pushed into non-training roles through lack of posts.

Calling this “moaning” avoids fixing it

When doctors point out pay erosion or unsafe workloads, it is often dismissed as attitude rather than evidence. No other profession is expected to quietly absorb decade-long pay cuts.

Nothing changes without a valid ballot mandate

Industrial action is only lawful with a live ballot. Turnout is what gives leverage. A weak mandate is read as acceptance of the status quo.

Not voting has consequences

If turnout drops, negotiating power drops. Future pay deals are shaped by what is tolerated now. This affects you whether you are an F1, a registrar, or near CCT.

The reballot is the mechanism

Voting takes minutes. It is the only direct influence doctors have collectively. High turnout is the message that keeps negotiations alive.
